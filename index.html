0. Introduction: Life Goal—Being a Good Father

My primary life goal is to be a good father, ensuring my children grow up in a nurturing, culturally rich, and stimulating environment. For me, being a good father means having ample free time, financial security, and a flexible lifestyle that allows me to focus on my family and their development.

1. Financial Security and Career Plans

1.1. Degree in Environmental Biology

I am finishing my degree in Environmental Biology at the Autonomous University of Barcelona (UAB).

While I may not initially pursue a career in this field, the qualification could provide job opportunities in developed countries like Australia or Northern Europe.

When my children are older and seek more independence (around ages 16–18), I might revisit my career in biology, particularly if we have settled in a developed country by then.

1.2. Online Teaching

I have experience as an online teacher from my time in Sydney, where I taught a particular language.

This profession appeals to me because it pays well, offers the flexibility to work remotely, and fits well with my goal of maintaining free time for my family.

It allows me to adapt to living in different places without compromising my income.

1.3. Investment Plans

I plan to invest in Indonesia with a colleague. We estimate that within a few years, the investment will yield returns.

This investment could provide us with a steady income equivalent to at least a part-time monthly minimum wage or more.

The combination of investment returns and earnings from online teaching, even if my client base remains modest, would enable us to live comfortably, especially in countries with lower living costs.

Living in such countries would allow me to save most of my earnings, building up financial security for future plans.

2. Lifestyle and Cultural, Linguistic, and Religious Appreciation

2.1. Value of Cultural, Linguistic, and Religious Diversity

I place great importance on immersing myself and my family in culturally diverse environments. This includes not only languages but also understanding different religions, traditions, and practices.

Language is an integral part of culture, and I believe my children should experience and learn various languages as they immerse in different cultural contexts.

My own background (likely including my wife's different cultural and religious background) will expose our children to multiple perspectives, fostering an appreciation for diversity.

For instance, if we lived in Senegal, our children would learn Wolof, Serer, and French, in addition to Catalan, Spanish, and any languages my wife and I speak.

In countries like India, they could potentially speak five languages alongside our own foreign languages, gaining exposure to multiple religious backgrounds, including my own and my wife's.

2.2. Living in Multilingual and Multicultural Countries

I am particularly drawn to multicultural and multilingual countries. These countries offer opportunities for my family to experience a wide range of traditions, practices, and ways of living.

Being exposed to different cultural and religious practices enriches our lives, helping my children grow up with open minds and an appreciation for global diversity.

2.3. Seasonal Living Arrangements

If we reside in a tropical or agriculturally focused country, our schedule would align with the seasonal rhythms typical of primary sector economies, which are often busier during certain times like planting or harvest seasons.

For instance, in Sub-Saharan Africa, some schools operate around these peak times, and we could adapt our lifestyle accordingly, ensuring our children benefit from local education while engaging in cultural practices.

We would also take advantage of these busy periods to spend a few months each year in another location, such as Mexico, Spain, or my wife's home country. This arrangement would allow for intensive immersion in family life during these months and create a balanced, rich experience for our children.

3. Parenting Philosophy

3.1. Learning by Offering, Not Imposition

I strongly believe in fostering learning opportunities by providing resources and experiences rather than imposing them.

For instance, if we have musical instruments, sports equipment, tools for trades like engineering, a garden, a kitchen, pets, and so on at home, our children will have the freedom to explore and develop interests on their own.

If they show interest, such as wanting to learn piano because they see me or my partner play occasionally, then we support them by enrolling them in lessons. This approach ensures that their motivation is genuine.

3.2. Providing Diverse Stimuli at Home

Our home would be filled with a variety of stimuli, including musical instruments, sports equipment, tools for engineering and trades, a garden, a kitchen, pets, etc. This comprehensive environment will provide many opportunities for our children to discover their interests and talents.

Exposing them to different activities, such as gardening, cooking, woodworking, and sports, allows them to engage with diverse life skills.

3.3. Balancing Strict Cultural Norms with a Relaxed Home Environment

I believe in taking advantage of local cultural norms that may be stricter or more disciplined than those I impose at home. In some countries, school systems or societies may be strict, valuing discipline and academic achievement.

By offering a more relaxed atmosphere at home, I create a space where my children can unwind, feeling less pressure and more support from me as a parent.

This approach allows me to be their comfort zone and confidant, someone they trust and rely on. It also helps in building a stronger, closer relationship, encouraging them to open up about their personal lives.

With this bond, I can better understand their needs and challenges, helping me to guide and support them effectively while earning their respect and appreciation.

4. Future Plans for Children's Education and Development

4.1. Living Environment

I envision us living in a spacious house in a rural, inland town. A large space would accommodate the diverse activities and stimuli we wish to provide for our children, ensuring they have ample room to explore and learn.

4.2. Moving During Adolescence

When our children reach around 13 years of age, we would consider relocating to a developed country. This transition would allow them to access better educational opportunities and a more advanced schooling system.

I would carefully research countries that offer strong educational systems and a quality of life that align with our family values.

4.3. Potential Countries

Australia: I am particularly interested in Australia due to its strong economy and high-quality educational system. It would provide opportunities for me to re-enter the workforce, possibly restarting my biology career when my children are more independent.

Other Options: Depending on our experiences and preferences, other developed countries might also be suitable for this transition.

4.4. Multilingual and Multicultural Education

As we move between countries, our children will naturally acquire multiple languages and gain exposure to various cultural and religious backgrounds.

For instance, living in the Republic of Congo would mean learning Lingala, Monokutuba, and French, while living somewhere might expose them to even more languages and cultural contexts. They would also maintain our own family languages, Catalan and Spanish, along with any others from my wife's background.

This linguistic and cultural diversity ensures they become well-rounded, open- minded individuals capable of navigating different societies and appreciating varied traditions.

5. Importance of Shared Life Goals in Relationships

5.1. Avoiding Forced Changes

It's essential not to force life changes or alter personal goals solely for a relationship, as doing so could lead to frustration or dissatisfaction later in life.

If one changes their aspirations or expectations for the sake of a partner, it can create a future conflict, particularly if those changes aren't in harmony with the partner's own goals.

5.2. Ensuring Alignment in Life Goals

Partners must have aligned goals and foundational agreements to build a stable and healthy relationship. Initially, the infatuation phase may overshadow fundamental differences, making it easy to overlook these crucial aspects.

When the initial excitement fades, discrepancies in life goals can resurface, potentially leading to the relationship's end. It's vital to discuss and align these aspects early on, ensuring both partners are committed to similar objectives.

6. Conclusion

My life plan is deeply rooted in being a present and supportive father, achieved through financial stability, flexible work, and immersion in culturally and linguistically diverse environments. By combining investment income and teaching work, I aim to maximize time with my family while saving for the future.

Providing a home environment rich in opportunities for learning—through music, trades, cooking, and outdoor activities—ensures my children develop in a stimulating and supportive space. Additionally, embracing cultural, linguistic, and religious diversity enriches our lives and broadens our horizons.

Ultimately, the plan balances financial independence, cultural appreciation, and personal freedom, creating a fulfilling and stable family life that remains true to our shared aspirations and values.
